Our Chapter

Both State High TSA chapter 9/10 and 11/12 are part of PATSA Region 8 and have participated in TSA for many years. We are located in State College, Pennsylvania and attend State College Area High School, a historically high-performing school with a 93% high school graduation rate. Our school routinely achieves impressive performances in TSA, sending students to States and then Nationals every year. We encourage students to engage in community service projects and operate with a "Education First" attitude. One way we do this is by advertising certain community service projects such as a 5k to help fight cancer.

Both State High chapters strive to follow the TSA motto of "Learning to Lead In A Technological World."

We accomplish this by working as a team at weekly practices to develop the best understanding of various aspects of STEM. In addition, we elect a President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer, Reporter, and Sergeant-at-Arms to develop and practice leadership skills.

The developers of this website wish to thank Mr. Olmstead!

Mr. Olmstead is the TSA 9/10 chapter advisor at State College Area High School. Throughout our TSA experience this year, he has encouraged us to act ethically, do well in TSA, and follow the TSA motto.

Career and Technical Education (CTE) at State College Area High School

Career and Technical Education is offered at State High as part of its outstanding Career and Technical Center (CTC) where students can explore topics of interest and prepare them for their future. Specific subjects offered in our CTC program are:

  • Accounting and Finance

    • In this program

  • Agricultural Science

    • In this program students focus on new and upcoming agricultural technology in order to prepare them to help with critical agriculture issues society is facing.

  • Architectural Drafting and Design

    • In this program students learn the essentials required in order to design structures such as our new high school building, bridges, and houses.

  • Automotive Technology

    • In this program students learn the mechanics of cars so that they can join the automotive industry in the future

  • Building Construction

    • In this program students learn what it takes to be a builder which gives them a stup up when it comes to the real world of construction.

  • Cisco Networking Academy

    • In this program students learn all about computers in order to prepare them for whatever computer related issues may come there way in the future.

  • Engineering

    • In this program students can focus on different engineering majors such as robotics or electrical in order that they may be able to focus in those areas in the future.

    In addition to this comprehensive selection, CTE is integrated into the high school's general STEM curriculum.

    State College Area High School also offers many extracurricular activities that are STEM oriented. In addition to TSA, there are the following clubs:

    What does this all mean?

    The number and variety of STEM and CTE courses and activities avaliable at State High allows students to pursue their own interests whatever they may be. These courses also ensure that all students can get a solid foundation in the field of their choice. That being said, our school district realizes that High School alone is not enough time to teach STEM so alot of STEM is taught at the middle schools. Specifically:

  • Important scientific skills such as the scientific method and safety.
  • Woodworking.
  • 3D printing.
  • Basic coding.
  • Problem solving.

    • Through all of this, State High's CTE program prepares students for life after school and ensures that they will have a good chance to achieve their goals.
