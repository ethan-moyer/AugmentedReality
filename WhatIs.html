What is it?

The Oxford Living Dictionary defines augmented reality as, “A technology that superimposes a computer-generated image on a user's view of the real world, thus providing a composite view.” Augmented reality can take several different forms: from software that overlays images using a phone's camera, to wearable devices such as a head mounted display or glasses. Any of these can be considered augmented reality, but it is important to make a distinction between augmented reality and virtual reality. Virtual reality simulates an entirely new environment, while augmented reality only adds simulated objects or alterations to an environment that already exists.
